Advanced Assumptions

Expand or collapse advanced settings

At-fault claim counts are modeled with a Poisson distribution, and the core input F is used directly as the Poisson parameter λ, so F = E[K] = λ.

K ~ Poisson(λ) F = 35.0% λ = 0.3500 E(K) = λ = F
\[ F = E[K] = \lambda \]
\[ \Pr(K=k) = e^{-F}\frac{F^k}{k!} \]
\[ \Pr(K \ge 1) = 1 - e^{-F} \]
F is entered as a percentage but used internally in decimal form, so 35% means F = 0.35. To support the regional lower bound and an exemption cap of up to M = 4, the matrix enumerates \(K=0..14\) exactly and pools the tail mass for \(K \ge 15\) into level 5.
